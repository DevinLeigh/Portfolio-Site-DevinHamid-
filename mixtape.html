Mix Tape

Mix Tape is a music player mobile app that plays music and videos from the 1980s. This app will allow a user to create a profile, search for artists, and curate playlists. I decided the UI should feel reminiscent of the music devices used in the 80s, evoking fun and nostalgia while remaining intuitive and user-friendly.

My Process

Research

First, I studied and compared 3 similar music apps to get an idea of what elements might be most helpful to include, as well as looking for design principles and patterns found in each.

Ideation

Low-fidelity wireframes were sketched on paper, helping to solidify and create the overall look, feel, and flow of the design I wanted.

Wireframes

Using what I learned about grids, layout, and spacing, the low-fidelity wireframes were made into mid-fidelity wireframes using Adobe XD.

Design

I designed and built a set of cohesive icons from scratch using vector shape tools.

Finally, the text styles and color palette were defined and could then be added to the design to create the final high-fidelity wireframes.

Mockups

Adding the final frames to mockups illustrates the overall design of the app and how it will appear in “real life.” Even though only 6 pages were created, it’s easy to understand how this app would look to the user.

Conclusion

This was a smaller project, intended to give me practice with fundamental elements of design. The task was to create a UI direction that would work for branding and digital UI of the product for a target audience age range that I fall into, so I set out to create a useful, fun product that I would personally enjoy using. With that as the benchmark, I feel this project was very successful.
